# Onie — Brand Name & Meaning

Canonical reference for why the product is called **Onie**, what the name connects to across languages, and how we talk about it in copy.

## The name

**Onie** (pronounced *OH-nee* — close to Polish *oni* [ˈɔ̃ɲi])

A short, spellable wordmark for a public feed where practitioners publish the agent workflows they run in their own work.

---

## Primary meaning — Slavic *oni* (“they / them”)

The strongest anchor. **Oni** is the third-person plural pronoun — “they” — across a large family of languages:

| Language | Word | Meaning | Pronunciation |
|----------|------|---------|---------------|
| Russian | они | they / them | [ɐˈnʲi] |
| Polish | oni | they (mixed-gender group) | [ˈɔ̃ɲi] |
| Czech | oni | they | [ˈonɪ] |
| Slovak | oni | they | [ˈɔni] |
| Serbo-Croatian | oni | they | [ôni] |
| Slovene | oni | they | [ˈóːni] |

**Why it fits the product:** Onie is social. Workflows come from **them** — peers in the field — not from an anonymous prompt dump. The name points at collective practice.

**Primary line:**

> Onie — where they publish how they work.

**Secondary lines:**

> Workflows from practitioners, not playbooks from nowhere.  
> A feed of what *they* run — tagged by discipline and stack.

The extra **e** in *Onie* keeps the wordmark distinct as a product name while staying phonetically close to Polish *oni* and the broader Slavic form.

---

## Secondary meaning — Scots *onie* (“any”)

In **Scots** and northern English dialect, **onie** is a historical spelling of **any**:

- *onie body* — anybody  
- *onie place* — anywhere  
- *onie thing* — anything  

Etymology: same root as English *any* → Old English *ǣnig* (“one-y”) → one of many, no matter which.

**Why it fits the product:** Explore is open and cross-disciplinary — any field, any tool, any workflow worth studying.

**Supporting line:**

> Any discipline. Their practice.

---

## Tertiary meaning — Japanese 鬼 (*oni*)

**鬼 (oni)** — a figure from Japanese folklore: a powerful spirit, often translated as ogre or demon. In compounds it also carries force and skill (e.g. 鬼のように — “like an oni,” i.e. intensely).

**Use in brand:** Optional visual and tonal layer — strength, craft, something you respect — not “evil” or horror. Fine for mark and illustration; do not lead with “demon” in English copy.

---

## Tertiary meaning — Yoruba *oní-* / *òní*

Two relevant readings:

1. **òní** — *today*. Suggests current, living practice (what people run now).
2. **oní-** — prefix meaning *owner, possessor, agent of* (e.g. *onílé* = landlord, *onílù* = drummer). Denotes the person who has or does the thing.

**Use in brand:** Background depth only. The “practitioner who owns the workflow” idea aligns with the product but is too abstract for a headline without explanation.

---

## Other *oni* forms (not primary)

| Language | Meaning | Note |
|----------|---------|------|
| Welsh | unless / until | Grammatical particle |
| Māori | to thrust forward | Unrelated |
| Lindu (Philippines) | noise | Obscure |

Do not build brand story on these.

---

## How the spellings relate

| Form | Role |
|------|------|
| **Onie** | Product name — wordmark, URL, app |
| **oni** | Slavic pronoun; Japanese 鬼; Yoruba prefix |
| **onie** | Scots dialect spelling of *any* |

**Onie** deliberately blends: sounds like Slavic *they*, reads like Scots *any*, stands alone as a product name.

## Brand story (approved framing)

Use **collective / Slavic** as the lead. Layer **Scots any** in subcopy when breadth matters.

### One sentence

> **Onie** is a feed of agent workflows from practitioners in the field — what they run, how it is wired, and which tools it depends on.

### Elevator (with name etymology)

> **Onie** takes its name from *oni* — “they” in Russian, Polish, and related languages. It is where practitioners share the setups they use in their own work: prompts, skills, file layouts, tagged by discipline and stack.

### Short taglines

- Where they publish how they work.  
- Any discipline. Their practice.  
- Agent workflows from people in the field.

### What we avoid in naming copy

- Leading with Japanese “demon” or horror tropes  
- Over-explaining every language layer in the hero  
- Invented etymology or claims we cannot support  
- Positioning Onie as a made-up syllable with no roots

## Usage guidelines

| Context | Name form |
|---------|-----------|
| Product, UI, wordmark | **Onie** |
| Title tag | `Onie — Agent workflows from people doing the work` |
| Body copy (first mention) | **Onie** (bold once if needed) |
| Possessive | Onie’s feed, Onie’s Explore |
| Verb | Do not verb (“onie it”) |

**Do not** rename to Weavel in user-facing copy unless doing a deliberate product pivot. Weavel remains the repo / infra name in some places; **Onie** is the brand.

---

## Honest caveats

- Cyrillic **они** is spelled *oni*, not *onie* — the link is phonetic and Slavic-family, not a literal Russian spelling.  
- Japanese **oni** will be the first association for some audiences (anime, games). That is acceptable; it is not the story we lead with.  
- Scots **onie** is dialect; most English speakers will not know it without a footnote. Use as a supporting layer, not the main hook.
