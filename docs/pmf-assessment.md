# Onie PMF Assessment

## Current verdict: Pre-PMF (too early to score)

The Sean Ellis benchmark requires **40%+ "very disappointed"** responses to: *"How would you feel if you could no longer use this product?"*

**Onie cannot be scored yet.** Reasons:

- Fewer than 30 active users (demo seed accounts only in production)
- Users have not had sustained time to integrate Onie into weekly workflows
- No PMF survey has been run

Treat this doc as the **measurement plan** and **pre-PMF signal checklist**, not a score.

## PMF survey — run when ready

### When to run

- At least **30 users** who have used Onie for **2+ weeks**
- Mix of publishers and lurkers (both matter)
- After a user has browsed Explore, followed someone, or published at least once

### Core question (Sean Ellis)

> How would you feel if you could no longer use Onie?

- Very disappointed
- Somewhat disappointed
- Not disappointed

**PMF threshold:** ≥40% "Very disappointed"

### Follow-up questions (Rahul Vohra method)

1. What type of people do you think would benefit most from Onie?
2. What is the main benefit you receive from Onie?
3. How can we improve Onie for you?
4. (Optional) What would you use instead if Onie disappeared?

### Segment analysis

Break results by:

- **Field** (UX, SaaS, science, engineering, etc.)
- **Role** (publisher vs. consumer)
- **Entry point** (Explore search vs. follow feed vs. direct link)

The segment with the highest "very disappointed" % is your **beachhead** — double down there before broadening.

### Roadmap from survey (Superhuman engine)

1. Filter to users who said "very disappointed"
2. Cluster their "how to improve" responses
3. Prioritize improvements that **both** very-disappointed users want **and** somewhat-disappointed users would convert on
4. Re-survey quarterly; track % very disappointed over time

## Honest pre-PMF diagnosis

### What's working (product shape)

- Clear positioning: practitioner workflows, not generic prompts
- Right primitives: field tags, tools, follow feed, explore, fork attribution
- Brand narrative ("where they publish how they work") matches the social feed model
- Competitive white space vs. registries and team tools

### What's not proven yet

- **Supply:** Are real practitioners willing to publish their setups publicly?
- **Demand:** Will people return weekly to browse workflows, or is this a one-time novelty?
- **Differentiation felt by users:** Do users perceive Onie as different from skills.sh / X threads, or just another place to post?

### Highest-leverage PMF experiments (next 4 weeks)

1. **Beachhead outreach** — recruit 10 practitioners in one field (e.g. UX research or SaaS PM) to publish one real workflow each
2. **Fork CTA** — make "use this workflow" (fork) prominent on post pages; measure fork rate
3. **Weekly digest** — email followers when someone they follow publishes (tests retention loop)
4. **PMF interviews** — 5 calls with target users before survey; ask what they used before Onie and why they'd come back

## Decision framework

| PMF score (when measurable) | Action |
|---|---|
| <20% very disappointed | Pivot beachhead segment or core loop |
| 20–40% | Iterate on top survey complaints; narrow ICP |
| ≥40% | Double down on acquisition in winning segment |
| ≥40% and growing | Scale distribution (SEO, integrations, partnerships) |
